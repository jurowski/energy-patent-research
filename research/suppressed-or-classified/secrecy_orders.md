# Patent Secrecy Orders (35 U.S.C. §181)

## How it works
1. Inventor files patent application with USPTO
2. USPTO routes application to relevant defense agencies for review
3. If deemed "detrimental to national security," a secrecy order is issued
4. Inventor is prohibited from publishing, disclosing, or filing foreign patents
5. Violation is a federal crime (up to 2 years imprisonment)
6. Orders are renewable indefinitely

## Key facts
- As of ~2023, approximately 5,900+ active secrecy orders
- New orders issued: roughly 100-200 per year
- Most are in cryptography, nuclear, and military tech
- BUT: some energy-related patent classes are affected
- Inventors receive NO compensation unless they successfully petition

## Relevant patent classes to investigate
These IPC/CPC classes may have elevated secrecy order rates:
- H02K — Dynamo-electric machines
- H02N — Electric machines not otherwise provided for
- H01M — Electrochemical cells / batteries
- F03G — Spring, weight, inertia, or similar motors
- G21 — Nuclear physics / nuclear engineering
